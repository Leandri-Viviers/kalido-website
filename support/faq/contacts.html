Contacts

What is a Kalido Contact?

Two users are Kalido Contacts if they are both on Kalido, have each otherʼs respective mobile numbers, and have not blocked each other. So, although youʼll match with a range of people (in decreasing degrees of relevance, and social and physical closeness to you), you need to exchange details with someone before they become a Kalido Contact.

Whatʼs the benefit of adding Kalido Contacts?

The more Kalido Contacts you have, the more referrals youʼll get, and the easier it will be to match you with potential clients and professionals. As Kalido ranks you based on your social closeness with someone, upgrading a user from a general contact (friend of a friend) to your Kalido Contact (friend) will push you up the ranks. To get more Kalido Contacts, invite your colleagues, friends, and family to join. Youʼll be connected to their contacts, which you can then upgrade to become your Kalido Contacts.

Can I introduce people to each other if theyʼre not on Kalido?

Yup. As long as a contact is in your phonebook, even if this person hasnʼt joined Kalido yet, you can make an introduction.

How can I be sure a contact really is as qualified as they say they are?

Kalido makes it easy to check someone out:

  • You can ask your common contacts for referrals, so you have an idea of their personality and reputation before meeting up.
  • Since youʼll likely be physically close to each other, you can always arrange for a face-to-face meet up before choosing to work with them.

Kalido doesnʼt vet or endorse any users, but we feel that letting you access someoneʼs shared contacts and networks gives you a better chance of assessing their qualities than relying on ratings or reviews from strangers.
