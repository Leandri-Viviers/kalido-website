Matching

What is a match?

A match is someone Kalido has found that fits your goal. So, itʼs the great guitar teacher youʼve been looking for. Or, if youʼre the guitar teacher, itʼs the dream client youʼve been wanting to meet.

How does matching work?

Kalido ranks matches based on how relevant, and socially and physically close they are to you. So, someone who lives close by (physically close) and shares the same goal as you (relevant) will rank higher than someone who shares the same goal, but is far away. And your friend, who is socially closer to you, will rank higher than a friend of a friend. Kalidoʼs personalized ranking of matches helps you make a more educated decision about the best person to work with.

How specific is Kalidoʼs matching?

Very. Your goal and the other personʼs goal must match. Only if Kalido canʼt find someone whose goal matches yours exactly, will users with related goals be suggested. Additionally, your matches are ranked for physical proximity, with people closest to you ranking higher, making it easier for you to meet up and work together.

How can I find the best match possible?

Thatʼs what Kalido is here for! You can help by making your goals as specific as possible (donʼt just tell us youʼre looking for an ‘accountant’, tell us you want a ‘forensic accountant who lives in Chicago’); making your profile as complete as possible (add a profile picture, media, and interests etc.); and inviting as many people as possible to join Kalido (so we can match you to their extended networks too).

Who will I match with?

Kalido will match you to someone who has a complementary goal to yours. So, your Provide Goal of ‘I can give yoga lessons’ will be matched to your clientʼs Meet Goal of ‘I want to meet a yoga teacher’. Because Kalido offers natural language searching, it doesnʼt matter what phrase you use (‘yoga teacher’ or ‘yoga lessons’), the system will intelligently match you.

Can I change whom Iʼm matched with?

Yes, you can change your settings to only match with people you know, people who have something in common with you (a shared contact or network, for example), or anyone on Kalido.

What if Iʼm not happy with the contact Iʼm matched with?

Kalido does not vet or endorse any users, which means that, unfortunately, we canʼt take responsibility for the quality of services rendered. Kalido gives you information that helps you decide whether you want to work with someone. Our personalized ranking system guides you towards contacts who are more relevant, and socially and physically closer to you, but ultimately, it will be your decision. However, if youʼve fallen victim to fraud or other illegal activities, please let us know, and we will certainly help you block someone, or remove their account from Kalido.

Can I match with a specific client?

No. Kalido will find you the best client available from all the users in the database though.

Can I match with a specific professional?

No. However, Kalido will find you the best professional available from all the users in the database. Although you wonʼt be matched to a specific person, youʼll still be matched with someone whose skills suit your goal.

Can I match with someone from a specific network?

Yes. For example, you can specify ‘I want to meet someone from Great Yoga Teachers International’ as a Meet Goal. However, youʼll only be matched to someone from this network if you have something in common with them (a shared contact or network), and if theyʼve opted in to be matched by network.

What if I only want to view matches for a specific goal?

On your Kalido homepage, tap the filter icon on the top left, then select the type of matches you want to see under ‘Goals’.

How do I contact a match?

You can chat to them immediately with the chat feature. If youʼre not sure how to start the conversation, use one of the handy icebreakers to give you a suggested opening line.

I might be travelling or moving around soon, can I match with someone in my future location?

Not at the moment.

Why was I matched with someone who doesnʼt fit any of my goals?

Occasionally, Kalido will match you to someone who has a common network or common contacts with you, if your networks match one of their goals. For example, if youʼre part of Great Yoga Teachers International, and a user tells us ‘I want to meet someone from Great Yoga Teachers International’, youʼll be suggested to them as a match (but only if they have some trust-based connection to you i.e. a common contact or common network). If you donʼt find this feature helpful or necessary, you can always tap on the 3 vertical dots on the top right of your Match Card and select ‘Donʼt match with this person again’ or ‘This is a poor match’.
